# Blueprint Score
*Version 1.0 — Initial draft. Scoring rubric will be refined with each system version based on intake data and operator outcomes.*

---

## Overview
The Blueprint Score is a 100-point assessment of a brand's current clarity, preparation, and ability to scale — evaluated through the lens of the LESARUSS operating system. It is not a judgment of the person. It is a diagnostic of the system.

A score of 100 means the brand is fully ready — LESARUSS can focus entirely on execution and amplification. A lower score is not failure. It is a map. It tells us exactly where to start building.

**Who receives the Blueprint Score:**
- **Tier 2 (Guided)** — Score is generated by the LESARUSS operator after the Door 2 guided session
- **Tier 3 (Done For You)** — Score is generated by the LESARUSS team after the audit and interview
- **Tier 1 (DIY)** — Does not receive a Blueprint Score. DIY clients receive their own self-rating from Door 2, Section 5. The operator-evaluated score is a Guided and DFY benefit.

**When it is delivered:**
After Door 2 closes. Before the Blueprint Build begins (Door 3). The score informs where the build starts and what gets prioritized first.

## Category 1 — Mission Alignment (20 points)
*This is the most weighted category because it is the most important filter. LESARUSS does not exist to grow any brand. It exists to grow brands that are working toward the betterment of people, communities, and the planet. Every brand that enters the LESARUSS portfolio becomes a case study for what the system can do. Those case studies need to represent what we stand for.*

**Aligned brand areas include (not exhaustive):**
- Education — K–12, adult learning, literacy, charter schools, mentorship, tutoring
- Community development — homelessness, housing, re-entry, social services
- Health and healing — mental health, wellness, holistic health, trauma recovery
- Food systems — food justice, sustainable agriculture, nutrition equity, food access
- Environmental — healing the planet, climate action, sustainability, conservation
- Empowerment — women, youth, BIPOC communities, economic mobility
- Creative economy — music, art, storytelling, cultural preservation
- Nonprofit and social enterprise — mission-driven organizations building for impact

**Scoring rubric:**

| Score | Level | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 17–20 | **Deep Alignment** | Brand is explicitly and primarily working toward positive societal impact in one or more aligned areas. This is the brand LESARUSS was built to serve. Strong candidate for portfolio and case study. |
| 12–16 | **Moderate Alignment** | Brand has a values-driven orientation but impact is secondary to commercial goals, or the mission is not yet clearly defined. Eligible for Guided and DFY. Alignment should be developed in the blueprint. |
| 6–11 | **Neutral** | Brand is ethically acceptable but has no meaningful connection to LESARUSS mission areas. Consumer product, lifestyle, or entertainment brands with no social dimension. Eligible for Guided — not prioritized for DFY or portfolio. |
| 0–5 | **Misaligned** | Brand operates in industries or models that conflict with LESARUSS values — including but not limited to: environmentally harmful industries, exploitative business models, products or content that degrade community wellbeing. **Auto-flag: ineligible for Guided and DFY. DIY access only.** |

**Operator note:** Mission Alignment is assessed through the operator's judgment based on Door 2 answers, brand research, and the client interview. If a brand scores 0–5 on this category, escalate to LESARUSS leadership before proceeding. Do not decline unilaterally — flag and confirm.

## Category 2 — Brand Clarity (18 points)

How clearly can this operator articulate who they are, who they serve, and why they exist?

| Criteria | Points | How to Score |
|---|---|---|
| One-sentence "what do you do" is specific and audience-focused | 0–4 | 4 = specific and compelling, 2–3 = present but vague, 0–1 = missing or unclear |
| Target audience is defined with meaningful specificity | 0–4 | 4 = clear person with real detail, 2–3 = general category, 0–1 = undefined |
| Differentiator is articulated (not just "quality" or "passion") | 0–4 | 4 = specific and defensible, 2–3 = surface level, 0–1 = missing |
| Brand voice and tone are defined or clearly demonstrated | 0–3 | 3 = defined and consistent, 1–2 = implied, 0 = undefined |
| Brand assets exist (logo, colors, fonts) | 0–3 | 3 = complete system, 1–2 = partial, 0 = none |

---

## Category 3 — Offer Strength (18 points)

Is the thing they sell clearly defined, priced, deliverable, and producing results for clients?

| Criteria | Points | How to Score |
|---|---|---|
| Flagship offer is clearly named and defined | 0–5 | 5 = airtight, 3–4 = mostly defined, 1–2 = vague, 0 = undefined |
| Pricing is established and stated | 0–4 | 4 = clear pricing, 2–3 = approximate, 0–1 = no pricing |
| Delivery process is described and repeatable | 0–5 | 5 = documented process, 3–4 = described but loose, 1–2 = vague, 0 = no process |
| Client outcome or transformation is clearly defined | 0–4 | 4 = specific and measurable, 2–3 = general, 0–1 = undefined |

---

## Category 4 — Revenue & Traction (14 points)

Has this offer been validated in the market? Have people paid for it?

| Criteria | Points | How to Score |
|---|---|---|
| Has generated revenue (any amount) | 0–4 | 4 = yes, 0 = no |
| Has paying clients or customers | 0–4 | 4 = yes with multiple, 2–3 = one or two, 0 = none |
| Monthly revenue level | 0–6 | 0 = $0 / 1–2 = under $1k / 3–4 = $1k–$5k / 5 = $5k–$10k / 6 = $10k+ |

---

## Category 5 — Content & Audience (12 points)

Is this brand showing up, building an audience, and producing content with any consistency?

| Criteria | Points | How to Score |
|---|---|---|
| Active on at least one platform | 0–3 | 3 = yes actively, 1–2 = occasionally, 0 = no |
| Posts at least weekly on primary platform | 0–3 | 3 = yes, 1–2 = inconsistent, 0 = no |
| Content is connected to brand and offer (not random) | 0–3 | 3 = yes consistently, 1–2 = sometimes, 0 = no connection |
| Has evidence of content that worked (engagement, leads, sales) | 0–3 | 3 = yes clear example, 1–2 = some evidence, 0 = none |

---

## Category 6 — Operations & Systems (10 points)

Does the infrastructure exist to actually run a business?

| Criteria | Points | How to Score |
|---|---|---|
| Has core tools in place (at least 3 categories: email, CRM, PM, payments, etc.) | 0–4 | 4 = 4+ tools, 2–3 = 2–3 tools, 0–1 = minimal or none |
| Has described a workflow or process (even informally) | 0–3 | 3 = yes, 1–2 = partial, 0 = no process documented |
| Has a team or a documented capacity plan | 0–3 | 3 = team exists, 1–2 = solo but planned, 0 = no plan |

---

## Category 7 — Direction & Focus (8 points)

Do they know where they are going and why? Are they aware of their own gaps?

| Criteria | Points | How to Score |
|---|---|---|
| Has a specific, measurable 90-day goal | 0–3 | 3 = specific and achievable, 1–2 = general, 0 = none |
| Can articulate a 12-month vision | 0–3 | 3 = clear vision, 1–2 = vague, 0 = none |
| Demonstrates self-awareness about gaps (not defensive) | 0–2 | 2 = honest and specific, 1 = some awareness, 0 = no awareness or avoidant |

---

## Score Ranges and What They Mean

| Score | Level | What It Means | LESARUSS Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **91–100** | **Scale Ready** | Dream client. The system is built. Everything is in place. | Execution and amplification. Push it out. |
| **76–90** | **Operating** | Strong system with minor gaps. Real growth is accessible now. | Optimization. Close the remaining gaps and scale. |
| **56–75** | **Developing** | Solid core, meaningful gaps. Traction is happening but the system is leaking. | Systematize what works. Patch the gaps. Create consistency. |
| **31–55** | **Building** | Some pieces exist but the system is incomplete. Good foundation to work from. | Fill critical gaps. Build the missing foundations. Establish rhythm. |
| **0–30** | **Foundation** | Major gaps in core areas. Starting from the ground up. | Build the basics first. No scaling before the foundation exists. |

## The Mission Alignment Gate

Regardless of overall score, any brand that scores **0–5 on Mission Alignment** is automatically flagged:

- **DIY access:** Permitted. The $11 product is a self-service tool and remains open.
- **Guided (Tier 2):** Ineligible. LESARUSS operator time is not allocated to misaligned brands.
- **Done For You (Tier 3):** Ineligible. LESARUSS will not take on a DFY engagement for a brand that conflicts with its values.
- **Portfolio / Case Study:** Ineligible. LESARUSS only features brands it is proud to represent.

**Operator instruction:** Flag to leadership. Do not communicate the flag to the client in those terms. If applicable, direct them to DIY resources and wish them well.

## Delivering the Score

The Blueprint Score is delivered to the client after Door 2, before the build begins. It should be framed as a starting point, not a verdict.

**Suggested framing:**

> *"Your Blueprint Score is [X]/100. Here's what that means and where we're starting..."*

Walk through:
1. Overall score and what range they're in
2. Their two highest-scoring categories (what they've built well)
3. Their two lowest-scoring categories (where the build starts)
4. How the score will be used to prioritize their blueprint

The score is also the **baseline for measuring progress**. Every 90 days (for Guided and DFY clients), a re-score is conducted. The goal over time is a rising score — not just better results, but a more complete, more capable operating system.

## Score Changelog
Every client score is logged in the LESARUSS system. Aggregate scoring data is reviewed quarterly and used to:
- Identify the most common gaps across all clients (informs next version priorities)
- Refine the rubric where operator scores are inconsistent
- Track whether the system is producing score improvements over time
